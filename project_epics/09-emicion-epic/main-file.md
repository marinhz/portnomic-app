Technical Specification: Eco-Compliance & Emissions Reporting ModuleProject: AgentSea AI (or your chosen brand)Module: AI-Driven Fuel & Emission AnalyticsCompliance Target: EU ETS (Emission Trading System) & FuelEU Maritime (2026 Standards)1. Executive SummaryThe goal of this module is to automate the extraction of fuel consumption data from non-structured sources (Captain’s Noon Reports, Arrival/Departure emails) and convert it into verified carbon emission reports. This solves the "regulatory headache" for ship owners and agents by ensuring data accuracy and minimizing the risk of EU financial penalties.2. AI Data Extraction (NLP Layer)The system must use Large Language Models (LLMs) to parse daily reports.2.1. Input SourceFormat: Plain text emails, PDF attachments, or Excel Noon Reports.Trigger: Automated via the Email Parser module once a "Noon Report" or "Bunker Report" is detected.2.2. Target Data Points (The Extraction JSON)The AI must extract and structure the following fields:Vessel Identification: Name and IMO Number.Fuel Type: VLSFO, LSMGO, MGO, LNG, Biofuels, etc.Consumption (MT): Amount of fuel consumed in the last 24 hours or since the last port.Operational Status: \* At Sea: (Cruising).At Berth: (Loading/Unloading - critical for 100% EU port taxation).At Anchor: (Waiting).Distance Travelled: To calculate carbon intensity.3. The Calculation Engine (C-Engine)The backend must apply mathematical formulas to the extracted data to calculate the environmental and financial impact.3.1. $CO_2$ CalculationUsing the standard formula:$$E = C \times f$$$E$: Total $CO_2$ emissions (Metric Tons).$C$: Fuel Consumption (Metric Tons).$f$: Emission Factor (e.g., ~3.114 for VLSFO).3.2. Financial Impact (EU ETS Integration)EUA Estimation: Calculate the number of EU Allowances (EUAs) required for the voyage.Cost Projection: Multiply EUA count by the current market price of Carbon (fetched via external API).4. Key Functional Features4.1. Anomaly Detection (AI Auditor)The system flags reports where:Consumption is physically impossible for the distance covered.Fuel types mentioned do not match the vessel's technical profile.Goal: Prevent submission of fraudulent or erroneous data to authorities.4.2. "One-Click" Verification ExportGenerates an EU MRV (Monitoring, Reporting, Verification) compliant report.Format: JSON/XML for direct submission or PDF for independent verifiers (DNV, Lloyd’s Register).5. UI/UX RequirementsEmissions Dashboard: A visual representation of the "Carbon Debt" per voyage.Compliance Status: A simple Green/Yellow/Red indicator showing if the vessel meets FuelEU Maritime intensity targets.Activity Logs: A history of all extracted data with links to the original email for audit purposes (Transparency).6.
